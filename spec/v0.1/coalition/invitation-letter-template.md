## The Proposal

Every high-risk AI system shall maintain a continuously updated, cryptographically verifiable record of:

- Training & Development — datasets, preprocessing, training configuration
- Safety & Alignment — classifiers, reward models, RLHF iterations
- Evaluations — benchmarks, red-team reports, adversarial tests
- Incidents & Corrections — failure to root cause to corrective action
- Thresholds — operational tripwires as immutable governance events
- Data Exports — fields exported, destination, authorization
- Civilian Data Overrides — cryptographic receipts with justification, authority, expiration
- Model Lineage — model identifier, parent model, deployment history

## Why This Matters

The EU AI Act's high-risk provisions apply August 2, 2026. The current consultation is the last opportunity to shape the technical documentation standard. Without a provenance requirement, transparency remains a trust-based narrative, not an evidence-based audit.
